Sports Games

Sports games let players become faster, stronger, and more skilled at their favorite sports by picking up a controller instead of putting in years of practice.

Decades of innovation have taken sports games from abstract representations to the nearly photorealistic graphics. A great example of more abstract sports games is Tennis for Two, arguably the first sports game, and some believe, the first video game overall.

This rudimentary game used an oscilloscope for a display instead of a television screen. Despite its basic nature, it hit on a winning idea. The simple ruleset and widespread familiarity with sports make them natural candidates for successful video games.

During the 80s, professional and amateur leagues around the world began licensing teams to these games, and players associations started negotiating name, image, and likeness rights. As the games became more professional and high fidelity, these big names pushed the genre to new heights, with legendary games like ESPN NFL 2k5 and NBA Jam still carrying massive reputations with avid gamers.

These two examples show the two biggest subsets of sports games: arcade and simulation. Arcade sports games like NFL Blitz and NBA Jam bring sports to cartoonish new heights with flaming balls, dunks from half court, and the like. Simulation sports games like the Football Manager and NBA 2k series work to give the player the most realistic sports experience possible.

As you may be able to guess, arcade sports games were most popular when arcade games ruled the world. As home consoles started to take over the market, players had more time to sit and play their sports games, and demanded a deeper experience. Simulation games added real-life sports aspects like roster management and even budgeting to enhance the player experience.

Today, sports games dominate the charts, with eight of the top 20 games of 2024 covering sports like baseball, basketball, American football, soccer, and even professional wrestling. Arcade sports games are now more popular with indie developers, as the big leagues have gone all in on simulation games.
