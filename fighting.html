Fighting Video Games

Fighting games can be seen as a 1v1 or a group vs another group. This type of genre not only was popular as other types of genreâ€™s, but it was a very controversial type of genre for how violent it was.

The first fighting video game is Heavyweight Champ (1976) which is an arcade game, but the fighting game that started the popularity is Street Fighter 1 & 2 (1987 & 1991).

After Street Fighter 2, a video game called Mortal Kombat (1992) came out which contributed to a rating system for how violent or other mature themes the video game had in the content.

Ever since the rating system has been established, all mainstream video games now have to have a content rating system of [E = Everyone, E10+ = All 10 and older, T = Teen, M = Mature, and A = Adults Only]. This rating system can be viewed similarly to how movies are rated for their audience.
